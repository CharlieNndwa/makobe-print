Makobe Print is founded upon service, and seeks to exceed clients' expectations in the quality of each printing project. Our commitment to excellence extends beyond the quality of finished product. The company was founded by the late Mr. Matsaneng Jeremiah "Jerry" Makobe in 1990 at the Small Business Development Centre, Pennyville, Industria, West of Johannesburg. During 1994, Mr. Matsaneng Jeremiah Makobe was awarded as entrepreneur of the month of August by the Sowetan newspaper. The SABC also featured a special documentary about Makobe Print on national television sharing the story of the humble beginnings of the company and the vision of Mr. Makobe. The documentary opened many doors for the company. During 2000 Eskom awarded Mr. Makobe a certificate of competence in business management.

Makobe Print's goodwill and reputation for excellence through quality service played a major role in the growth of the business. During 2004 Makobe Print moved from Industria to Braamfontein in Johannesburg. Late 2006, Mr. Matsaneng Jeremiah Makobe was involved in a tragic car accident and passed on. Makobe Print shut down premises in August 2009.

Today the sons of the late Mr. Matsaneng Jeremiah Makobe namely Thabo and Tshepo Makobe had reopened the company at their home in Three Rivers East, Vereeniging with the hope, enthusiasm, expertise and extensive experience in the printing industry to rebuild the legacy on the same principles that define the company .
